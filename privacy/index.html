Menuva Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 16 December 2025 Last updated: 16 December 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how Menuva handles personal data when you use the Menuva iOS app (the “App”) and related web pages we operate (together, the “Service”). Menuva is designed to be accountless and data-minimizing. However, some information (especially location data) can still be personal data under UK GDPR.

1.

Who is responsible for your data

  1. 1.1 Controller. Duke DJ Saputra (student-led project, “Menuva”).
  2. 1.2 Contact email. hello@menuva.co.uk
  3. 1.3 Contact address. University of Warwick, Scarman Rd, Coventry CV4 7AL, United Kingdom
  4. 1.4 If you contact us via email, we will process the information you include to respond.
2.

Intended territory

  1. 2.1 Menuva is intended for use in the United Kingdom only. If you use the Service outside the UK, you do so at your own risk and are responsible for complying with local laws.
3.

What data we collect

A. Location data (precise location)

  1. 3.A.1 If you grant iOS location permission, the App collects precise location to:
    1. identify nearby venues, and
    2. request the correct menus/content for your nearest venue.
  2. 3.A.2 How it works. Location is used on-device and is also sent to our backend to determine which venue/menu content to return.
  3. 3.A.3 We do not intentionally store your precise location in our databases.
  4. 3.A.4 You can disable location access at any time in iOS settings. If you deny location access, manual venue selection remains available.

B. Dietary and allergen preferences

  1. 3.B.1 If you set dietary/allergen preferences in the App, they are stored on-device only and are not synced to our servers.

C. Feedback (optional)

  1. 3.C.1 If you use our feedback form at menuva.co.uk/feedback, your submission may include:
    1. your feedback responses, and
    2. optional contact details (only if you choose to provide them).
  2. 3.C.2 If you include contact details, they are personal data.

D. Technical data (network-level)

  1. 3.D.1 When your device connects to our infrastructure (for example, to fetch menus), network communications necessarily involve technical data such as IP address and request metadata being processed by the service providers that deliver content.
  2. 3.D.2 We do not use this to identify you, and we do not build user profiles.

E. App Store metrics (aggregated)

  1. 3.E.1 Apple provides aggregated metrics (e.g., downloads) to developers via App Store tools. We may use these high-level metrics for pilot evaluation.
4.

What we do not collect (current pilot)

  1. 4.1 For the current pilot App, we do not require accounts and we do not intentionally collect:
    1. names, emails, or phone numbers (unless you voluntarily submit them in feedback or email us),
    2. advertising identifiers or “tracking” across apps and websites,
    3. in-app analytics SDK event streams (e.g., Firebase Analytics), or
    4. crash reporting SDKs (e.g., Crashlytics).
  2. 4.2 If this changes, we will update this Privacy Policy and (where appropriate) provide in-app notice. Apple also requires keeping App Privacy disclosures accurate.
5.

How we use your data and our legal bases

UK GDPR requires a lawful basis for processing.

Purpose Data Lawful basis
Provide location-based nearby venue discovery and load the correct menus Precise location Consent (you choose to enable location in iOS)
Provide the Service reliably and securely (content delivery, abuse prevention, troubleshooting) Technical/network data processed by service providers Legitimate interests (operating a secure, reliable service)
Receive and respond to feedback Feedback content; optional contact details Consent (you choose what to submit) and/or legitimate interests (improving the pilot)
Pilot evaluation reporting (high-level usage and adoption) Aggregated App Store metrics; aggregated venue-level counts (if produced) Legitimate interests (evaluating and improving the pilot)

You can withdraw consent for location processing at any time by disabling location permissions in iOS settings.

6.

Who we share data with

  1. 6.1 We share data only as needed to run the Service:

A. Google Firebase / Google Cloud (menus and images)

  1. 6.A.1 Menu and venue content is hosted using Google Firebase services on Google Cloud infrastructure. This means Google processes technical data necessary to deliver content and operate the platform.

B. Google Forms (feedback)

  1. 6.B.1 If you submit feedback via our Google Form, Google processes that submission as the form provider.

C. Apple

  1. 6.C.1 Apple processes App Store distribution and provides developers with aggregated metrics.

D. Restaurants/venues (aggregated only, on request)

  1. 6.D.1 We may share aggregated information with participating venues (for example, download numbers and active user counts where available). We do not share precise location data with venues.
  2. 6.D.2 We do not sell personal data.
7.

International transfers

  1. 7.1 Our service providers may process data outside the UK. Where required, transfers are protected using appropriate safeguards (such as contractual protections) consistent with UK data protection requirements.
8.

Data retention

  1. 8.1 We keep data only as long as needed:
    1. Precise location: not intentionally stored in our databases; used transiently to return the nearest venue/menu content
    2. On-device preferences: stored locally until you delete the App or reset preferences
    3. Feedback submissions: retained for pilot evaluation and reporting, then deleted or anonymized when no longer needed (typically within 24 months)
    4. Service-provider logs: may exist within third-party infrastructure for security and operational reasons and are retained per provider configuration
9.

Security

  1. 9.1 We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect data, including encryption in transit and at rest where supported by our infrastructure, and restricted administrative access.
10.

Children and location

  1. 10.1 The Service is intended for users 13+. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13.
  2. 10.2 Because the App can use geolocation, we keep geolocation use limited to what is needed for nearby venue discovery and menu loading.
11.

Your rights

  1. 11.1 Depending on your circumstances, you may have rights including access, deletion, correction, restriction, portability, and objection. We may need enough information to identify the data you’re asking about.
  2. 11.2 Because the App is accountless and stores preferences on-device, Menuva may not be able to identify or retrieve App-related data about you beyond:
    1. anything you submit via feedback, and
    2. any correspondence you send us by email.
  3. 11.3 You also have the right to complain to the UK supervisory authority (the Information Commissioner’s Office).
12.

Changes to this Privacy Policy

  1. 12.1 If we change how we process data (for example, adding analytics or crash reporting), we will update this policy and, where appropriate, provide an in-app notice.
